The Los Angeles Lakers

The Lakers were founded in 1946 originally as the Detroit Gems and played in the NBL, The National Basketball League.

Shortly after, in 1947, the Gems moved to Minneapolis, Minnesota and the team's name was changed to the Lakers to reflect the Minnesota state nickname, Land of 10,000 Lakes.

In 1961, the Minneapolis Lakers finally made the move to Los Angeles after several seasons of low attendance by fans.

The Showtime Lakers

The Showtime Lakers were headed by the consensus greatest point guard of all-time, Magic Johnson, alongside arguably the greatest center of all-time, Kareem Abdul Jabbar.

While the Showtime Lakers have a reputation of being a great team, they also had the accolades and accomplishments to back it up. They won 4 titles while they were together, no small feat by any means.

James Worthy, also a first-ballot hall-of-fame player, was apart of this historic Lakers squad. He posted career averages of 17.6 ppg, 3.0 assists, and 5.1 rebounds. More info about James Worthy's career can be found here.

The Modern-Era Lakers

The Lakers of now are headed by coach Luke Walton, on his third year on the job, alongside already proven superstar, Lebron James, and younger emerging stars, Lonzo Ball, Brandon Ingram, and Kyle Kuzma.

Lebron James is currently in his first year of a four year super-max contract. He's hoping to help bring anotehr title back to Los Angeles after a five year playoff drought brought upon by the retirement of former on-court superstar Kobe Bryant.

The aforementioned budding stars are learning quickly on the job how to conduct themselves both on and off the court. Defense has been a major issue for this squad in terms of learning rotations and defensive assignments such as the "No-Middle" defensive scheme.
